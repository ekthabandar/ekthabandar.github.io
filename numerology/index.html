📖 How Numerology Works

Numerology derives meaning from numbers calculated from your birth date and name. Two main systems exist — Pythagorean (Western) and Chaldean (Ancient Babylonian).

⚡ Pythagorean System (Western)

Developed by the Greek mathematician Pythagoras (~570 BC). Maps A–Z to numbers 1–9 sequentially. Most widely used system globally, especially in Western and Indian urban numerology.

🌙 Chaldean System (Ancient)

Originated in ancient Babylon/Chaldea, considered older and more mystically accurate. Numbers 1–8 are used (9 is sacred). Assignment is based on vibrational frequency rather than sequential order.

Note: Number 9 is omitted as a letter value in the Chaldean system — it is considered divine and only appears as a final result.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Your Life Path Number is the most important number in numerology. It is calculated by reducing your full birth date (day + month + year) to a single digit (or Master Number 11, 22, 33). For example, DOB 15-06-1990: Day 15 = 1+5=6, Month 06=6, Year 1990=1+9+9+0=19=1+9=10=1+0=1. Sum: 6+6+1=13=1+3=4. Life Path 4.
Pythagorean numerology assigns letters A–Z sequentially to numbers 1–9, repeating. It is the most widely used modern system. Chaldean numerology, originating in ancient Babylon, assigns letters based on vibrational frequency rather than alphabet order, uses numbers 1–8 (9 is sacred), and generally considers the name most commonly used (not full birth name). Chaldean is considered older and more mystically accurate by many practitioners.
For Pythagorean numerology, use your full birth name exactly as it appears on your birth certificate — including middle names. For Chaldean numerology, use the name you currently go by most often, since Chaldean focuses on current vibrations rather than birth-assigned energy. The Lo Shu Grid is a 3×3 magic square used in Chinese numerology and Vastu, where each position holds a number 1–9. Your birth date digits are plotted onto the grid — numbers present indicate strengths and energy in those life areas, while missing numbers reveal gaps to be developed. The grid pattern also reveals personality planes (mental, emotional, practical, intuitive).
Numerology identifies cycles, tendencies, and vibrations rather than predicting specific events. Numerology is best used as a self-awareness and planning tool, not as a deterministic forecast system.
